Compressive imaging systems acquire coded, low-dimensional projections and reconstruct the underlying scene computationally. Their practical performance depends on calibration accuracy, measurement quality, and efficient use of the sensing model.

This research line explores three complementary directions:

- **Measurement augmentation:** learning synthetic observations that improve the conditioning of reconstruction algorithms.
- **Computational calibration:** adapting trained reconstruction networks to individual measurements and sensing-model errors.
- **Structured computation:** exploiting the tensorial structure of coded spectral imagers to accelerate matrix operations.
